## Design Context

### Users
Brand leaders, agency creatives, marketing teams, and producers looking for a trusted production partner in a specific U.S. market. They need to evaluate relevant capabilities, see credible work, understand how Family Drama can execute locally, and start a commercial conversation quickly.

### Brand Personality
Cinematic, culture-forward, and premium. The experience should feel confident, editorial, contemporary, and creatively ambitious while keeping the work itself at the center.

### Aesthetic Direction
Preserve the existing Family Drama website exactly as the source of truth: an always-dark, image-led, Ritmica-driven visual system influenced by PRETTYBIRD and Tool of North America. New search landing pages may reuse the homepage's oversized typography, asymmetrical project imagery, restrained motion, black background, warm off-white type, and minimal navigation. Do not restyle, restructure, or visually simplify existing pages. Avoid generic SEO templates, card-heavy layouts, stock photography, bright SaaS palettes, and visual patterns that look machine-generated.

### Design Principles
1. Protect the existing site: new work must be additive and must not change current page layouts or interactions.
2. Let proof lead: pair search-focused copy with real Family Drama projects, directors, capabilities, and contact paths.
3. Keep SEO structure editorial: headings, FAQs, breadcrumbs, and internal links must feel composed rather than bolted on.
4. Make every location page useful: use market-specific context and honest service availability instead of city-name substitution.
5. Preserve speed and access: use semantic HTML, reduced-motion support, keyboard-visible controls, responsive layouts, and WCAG 2.2 AA contrast.
